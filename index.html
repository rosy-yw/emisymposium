About the symposium

An international forum on EMI, GenAI, and academic futures

Join us for the CUHK–UCL English-medium instruction (EMI) Symposium, to be held on 24 April 2026 at the Department of Curriculum and Instruction, The Chinese University of Hong Kong. Under the theme English-medium instruction in the era of GenAI: Negotiating tradition and modernity, the symposium brings together leading voices from Hong Kong and the international research community to examine how EMI is being transformed by generative AI at every level of higher education. Across keynote addresses and featured presentations, the event will explore questions of human agency, authorship, participation, assessment, teacher language awareness, academic language proficiency, research practice, and educational inequality. Rather than treating GenAI as either a simple solution or a passing disruption, the symposium creates a rigorous space for rethinking EMI in relation to multilingual realities, pedagogical responsibility, and the changing conditions of academic work.

Symposium themes

Thematic strands

Agency and decision-making

How should human judgement be protected when AI increasingly enters classroom interaction, instructional design, and academic work?

Participation and authorship

What happens to academic voice, collaboration, and visible participation when students work in hybrid human–AI environments?

Teacher development

How can AI support EMI teachers in developing stronger language awareness, scaffolding practices, and assessment design?

Preparedness and inequality

How do language support, institutional design, and AI-mediated practices shape access, inclusion, and stratification in EMI settings?

Academic literacy and assessment

How should we rethink proficiency, oral interaction, and academic literacy when new tools mediate both communication and evaluation?

Research ethics and methodology

What kinds of qualitative and interpretive practices remain necessary as open science and AI reshape scholarly expectations?
